Cocker Spaniel

Cocker Spaniels are dogs belonging to two breeds of spaniel dog type: the American and English Cocker Spaniels.

Historically, Cocker Spaniels were used for hunting. This is where the word "Cocker" originally comes from.

History

They are commonly assumed to have originated in Spain. In The Master of Game Edward says that Spaniel

are "Another kind of hound there is that be called hounds for the hawk and spaniels, for their kind cometh from Spain, notwithstanding that there are many in other countries."

Modern Breeds

English Cocker Spaniel

The UK calls these simply Cocker Spaniels, however they were recognized as a separate breed in 1946.

These dogs range from 15-16 inches and weigh 28-32 pounds. They are known as the most popular and successful dog breed in dog shows. This is typically due to the success of the dog owner. They are very popular in the UK, second most popular in fact. There are actually differences between the show dogs and the working dogs in the uk. The show dog is bred to the conformation standard and the working strain is bred for working ability.

American Cocker Spaniel

American cocker spaniels are generally a smaller breed. The European relatives are a little bit larger. They have a more stylish appearance and are in between 14.5 and 15.5 inches. They weigh between 24 to 30 pounds.
